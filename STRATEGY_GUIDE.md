## When to Use Each

### Use Balanced GPP When:
‚úì Standard main slate tournament
‚úì Contest size 10K-50K
‚úì You want a solid base strategy
‚úì Field is fairly balanced

### Use Leverage Play When:
‚úì You have strong ownership projections
‚úì You identify 3-4 clear leverage spots
‚úì Field is overreacting to narratives
‚úì You trust your projections

### Use Contrarian Core When:
‚úì Massive field (100K+ entries)
‚úì Chalky slate (lots of >30% owned plays)
‚úì You need significant differentiation
‚úì You have unique angle/info

### Use Ceiling Chaser When:
‚úì Milly Maker, Sunday Million contests
‚úì Prize pool extremely top-heavy
‚úì You're willing to accept variance
‚úì Min-cash doesn't matter

### Use Correlation Stack When:
‚úì High over/under game (50+ points)
‚úì Game has leverage opportunity
‚úì You can get full stack at good ownership
‚úì Bring-back available

## Common Mistakes by Strategy

### Balanced GPP Mistakes
‚ùå Too much chalk (4-5 players >25%)
‚ùå Not enough chalk (0-1 players >25%)
‚ùå No clear differentiation points

### Leverage Play Mistakes
‚ùå Chasing leverage at wrong positions (DST)
‚ùå Fading elite plays just for leverage
‚ùå Trusting bad ownership projections

### Contrarian Core Mistakes
‚ùå Being contrarian everywhere (all <10%)
‚ùå No anchor plays for stability
‚ùå Picking bad players just because low-owned

### Ceiling Chaser Mistakes
‚ùå All chalk thinking chalk = ceiling
‚ùå All low-owned punts
‚ùå No correlation/stacking

### Correlation Stack Mistakes
‚ùå Stacking wrong games (low totals)
‚ùå Over-stacking (5+ from one game)
‚ùå Missing the bring-back

---

**Remember:** No single strategy is always best. Choose based on:
1. Contest characteristics (size, payout structure)
2. Slate dynamics (chalky vs balanced)
3. Your edge (projections, ownership reads)
4. Your risk tolerance
